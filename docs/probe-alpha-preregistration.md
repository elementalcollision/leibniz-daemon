# Probe α — zero-LLM enumeration audit (pre-registration)

**Purpose.** The 7-model witness round narrowed our cross-backend conclusion: the wall may be the
*producer's* catalogue bias (an LLM recites Thue-Morse / Motzkin), not finite-encodability. This probe
**removes the LLM from the producer** and asks whether the *decided* region of a Walnut-decidable theory
contains a sound, faithful, non-trivial, **non-catalogued** theorem the LLM never proposed. It is the
liquidation audit of our own slogan, pre-registered so the result is honest either way.

## Pre-registered space (fixed; deterministic — no RNG, no LLM)
Generated by `scripts/enumerate_walnut_probe_alpha.py` (reproducible byte-for-byte):
- **Sequences:** fixed points of **uniform** k-morphisms over an m-letter alphabet, for
  `(k,m) ∈ {(2,2),(2,3),(3,2)}`, with `h(0)[0]=0` (fixed point from 0), **deduped up to alphabet
  relabeling**. (Uniform only — `promote` assigns numeration `msd_k` to a uniform k-morphism, so the
  decided statement is exactly about the real fixed point in the right base; non-uniform would mis-base.)
- **Filters (triage, before emitting):** drop **eventually-periodic** prefixes (degenerate) and ones
  whose fixed point **collapses to fewer letters**; **flag** catalogued words (Thue-Morse,
  period-doubling) — flagged claims are run but are *excluded from the novelty-candidate set*.
- **Properties:** **e-th-power-freeness** for `e ∈ {2,3,4}` (square-/cube-/4th-power-free), emitted as
  the exact Walnut predicate `?msd_k A i,p (p≥1) => (E t (t<(e-1)*p) & W[i+t]!=W[i+t+p])`.
- **Current sweep:** 166 deduped morphisms → 48 kept (aperiodic, non-degenerate) × 3 exponents = **144
  decided claims** (1 named-flagged: Thue-Morse). `--max-morphisms` truncates deterministically.

## Roles (trust boundary intact)
- **Producer:** exhaustive enumeration. **No LLM.**
- **Decider:** Walnut (sound; operator-run — the agent is sandbox-blocked from the external Java).
- **Faithfulness:** ~0 gap — the morphism *is* the definition; no English to mis-encode.
- **Novelty:** the **human blind panel** (invariant 4 — never an LLM/agent). An agent may run a
  *literature-retrieval advisory* to triage, but the novelty verdict is the panel's.

## Validate-syntax-first (measure-before-build, applied to the plumbing)
Run `probe_alpha_smoke.txt` **first**. It is Thue-Morse with known answers: square-free **FALSE**,
cube-free **TRUE**, 4th-power-free **TRUE**. If the smoke results don't match, the emitted
syntax/numeration is wrong — fix before the full batch (this is the lesson from the Observatory
`no_result` debugging).

## GREEN / RED (the decision rule)
- **GREEN:** ≥1 decided-true claim that is (i) on a **non-named** morphism, (ii) **aperiodic**
  (non-degenerate), (iii) survives a **literature/OEIS** check (not already catalogued), and (iv) is
  **blind-graded non-textbook** by the human panel — i.e. the LLM never proposed it and it is not in the
  books. → the slogan "finitely-encodable = textbook" is **falsified**; the lever is **search**, and a
  finite-witness *record factory* (Probe β: codes / covering arrays) becomes the autonomous direction.
- **RED:** 0 such survivors after sweeping the bounded space → even absent LLM bias, the *decided* region
  of these mined decidable theories is human-exhausted. Autonomous discovery in decidable showcase
  domains **closes cleanly**; pivot wholesale to verification amplification.

## What counts (honest caveats, from the witnesses)
- A GREEN here is most likely **shallow** non-textbook (an un-named morphism with an unremarked
  power-freeness threshold), *not* deep discovery — but that is exactly what the slogan-falsification
  test needs (GLM: "won't be deep, but shatter the equivalence"). The panel decides if even shallow
  counts.
- Power-freeness is the property family; the sequence space (un-named morphisms) is the novelty source.
- Verification amplification is the strategic home **regardless** of α's result; α only decides whether a
  narrow search-based autonomous track lives alongside it.
