# Kernel-attested low-degree ovoids of Q⁺(7,q): Kantor ovoid at q=4, failure at q=8 (Bartoli–Durante–Grimaldi–Timpanella 2025)

**Date:** 2026-07-06 · **Track:** T5 (external audit) / T9 (external corpus) · **Tier:** audit ·
**EV:** verification-amplification · **Gate:** GREEN (exact GF(2ʰ) arithmetic; Lean 4.31 `decide`)

## What this is

An independent, exact-arithmetic confirmation of a 2025 result in a domain new to the ledger (**finite geometry
— ovoids of polar spaces**). An **ovoid** of the hyperbolic quadric `Q⁺(7,q)` is a set of `q³+1` pairwise
non-collinear points; such ovoids are parametrized by three functions `f₁,f₂,f₃ ∈ F_q[x,y,z]`. Bartoli, Durante,
Grimaldi & Timpanella ([arXiv:2502.02219](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.02219), 2025) study the low-degree case. The
**Kantor ovoid** (`q = 2ʰ`) is described, for `q ∈ {2,4,16}`, by the degree-2 functions

$$f_1 = xy + z^2,\quad f_2 = xz + y^2 + z^2,\quad f_3 = yz + x^2 + y^2 + z^2,$$

and the paper records that at **`q = 8`** these same functions do **not** define an ovoid.

Whether they give an ovoid is **Condition (3)**: `O₇(f₁,f₂,f₃)` is an ovoid of `Q⁺(7,q)` iff for **all distinct**
`P₁=(x₁,y₁,z₁), P₂=(x₂,y₂,z₂)` in `F_q³`,

$$F := (x_1-x_2)\,(f_3(P_2)-f_3(P_1)) + (y_1-y_2)\,(f_2(P_2)-f_2(P_1)) + (z_1-z_2)\,(f_1(P_2)-f_1(P_1)) \ \neq\ 0.$$

## What Leibniz verified

By exact `GF(2ʰ)` arithmetic (`F₂[X]/(irreducible)`; characteristic 2, so `+ = − = XOR`; the census runs over
`F_q³ × F_q³`), with a field sanity check (associativity, distributivity, identity) in each case:

- **POSITIVE**: `q = 2` and `q = 4` give ovoids — every distinct pair has `F ≠ 0` (for `q=4`, all `64·63 = 4032`
  ordered distinct pairs); and `q = 16` likewise (the larger `≈16.7M`-pair census, `--full`);
- **NEGATIVE**: `q = 8` is **not** an ovoid — the explicit distinct pair `(0,0,0), (0,1,3)` has `F = 0`.

A single 2025 source thus yields **both** a positive (`q=4`) **and** a printed negative (`q=8`) — a built-in
cross-check on the encoding.

The **Lean 4.31 kernel** independently re-decides (plain `decide`, report-only) in

- `ovoid_q2` — Condition (3) holds for `q = 2`;
- `ovoid_q4` — Condition (3) holds for `q = 4` (the `4032`-pair census) ⟹ ovoid of `Q⁺(7,4)`;
- `ovoid_q8_fails` — the distinct pair `(0,0,0), (0,1,3)` has `F = 0` at `q = 8` ⟹ **not** an ovoid.

`#print axioms` shows at most `[propext]` for each — no `native_decide`, no `sorry`.

## Honest scope

This certifies the Kantor low-degree ovoid facts for the small cases the census reaches: `q = 2, 4` (positive,
in-kernel), `q = 8` (negative, in-kernel witness), and `q = 16` (positive, exact procedure with `--full`). The
paper's general theorems (e.g. the asymptotic non-existence for degrees `≥ 4` via Lang–Weil / Cafure–Matera, and
the classification of low-degree ovoids) are general results, not finite objects, and are not attempted here. The
backend is **report-only**: nothing sets `kernel_verified` or touches `trust.py`; `tests/test_invariants.py` is
byte-identical.
