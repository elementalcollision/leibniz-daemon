# Kernel-attested base case of the open Conjecture 4.1 (PΓU(5,q) non-spreading) — Bamberg–Giudici–Lansdown–Royle 2024

**Date:** 2026-07-06 · **Track:** T5 (external audit) / T9 (external corpus) · **Tier:** audit ·
**EV:** verification-amplification · **Gate:** GREEN (exact GF(q²) arithmetic; Lean 4.31 `decide`)

## What this is

An independent, exact-arithmetic verification of an **open** conjecture in finite geometry / classical groups —
a domain (finite polar spaces, non-spreading permutation groups) new to the ledger. In *Tactical decompositions
in finite polar spaces and non-spreading classical group actions* (Bamberg, Giudici, Lansdown & Royle; *Designs,
Codes and Cryptography* 2024; [arXiv:2403.17576](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.17576)), **Theorem 4.2** proves that
`PΓU(5,q)`, acting on the totally isotropic 1-spaces of the Hermitian polar space `H(4,q²)`, is **non-spreading**
— but only **conditional** on the following, still-open conjecture about counts of finite-field solutions:

> **Conjecture 4.1** (p.7). Let `q` be an odd prime power, `b ∈ F_{q²}` with `b^{q+1} = −1`, and
> `(s,u,w) ∈ F_{q²}³` with `s^{q+1} = u^{q+1} + w^{q+1}`. For `κ ∈ F_q^*`, let `n(κ)` be the number of
> `λ ∈ F_{q²} ∪ {∞}` such that
> `(wλ+1)(b+uλ)^q − (wλ+1)^q(b+uλ) = κ·(b² + 1 + λ²(s² − u² − w²))^{(q+1)/2}`.
> Then `n(κ) = n(−κ)` for all `κ ∈ F_q^*`.

## What Leibniz verified

By exact `GF(q²)` arithmetic — `F_{q²} = F_q[X]/(X²−r)`, `r` the least non-square mod `q`; Frobenius `x^q` negates
the `X`-coordinate; `λ ∈ F_{q²}∪{∞}` handled by homogenising `[λ:μ]` over the projective line `P¹(F_{q²})` (both
sides are degree-`(q+1)` homogeneous, so the test is projectively well-defined) — over the primes
`q ∈ {3, 5, 7}`, enumerating **every** admissible `b` and **every** `(s,u,w)` on the norm cone:

- **The symmetry `n(κ) = n(−κ)` holds** for every admissible `b` and every non-trivial `(s,u,w)` — zero
  violations across all three fields (`|valid (s,u,w)|` = 225 / 3025 / 16513).
- **The symmetry is specifically `κ ↦ −κ`**: for `q ≥ 5` there exist tuples with `n(1) ≠ n(2)` (`2 ≠ ±1`), so
  the conjecture is a genuine constraint, not the vacuous "all counts equal."

### A faithfulness finding: the sole exception is the trivial origin

Read *literally*, Conjecture 4.1 is false at exactly one triple: the **origin** `(s,u,w) = (0,0,0)`. This is the
zero vector — not a projective point — and in the paper's own derivation `(s,u,w)` arises from a **non-zero**
totally isotropic point `(−w,−u,t,u,w)`, so the origin is evidently outside the intended scope. Leibniz reports
this precisely: over all three fields the *only* failing triple is `(0,0,0)`, and with the non-degeneracy
`(s,u,w) ≠ 0` the conjecture holds with **zero** violations. (This pins the exact hypothesis; it is a triviality,
not a defect in the mathematics.)

## The Lean kernel re-decides the base case

The **Lean 4.31 kernel** independently re-decides the base case `q = 3` by plain `decide` (report-only), in
[`docs/crt/pgu_nonspreading.lean`](../crt/pgu_nonspreading.lean) — the field, the admissible parameter sets and
`P¹(F₉)` are all *generated by the kernel* from `(q,r) = (3,2)`, no baked data:

- `pgu_q3_symmetry` — `n(κ) = n(−κ)` for every admissible `b` and every **non-trivial** `(s,u,w)` on the norm
  cone;
- `pgu_q3_origin` — the trivial `(0,0,0)` **does** break the symmetry: a discriminating **negative control** that
  documents exactly why the non-degeneracy is load-bearing.

Both are `#print axioms`-clean — they **depend on no axioms at all** (pure `Nat`/`Bool`; no `native_decide`, no
`sorry`).

**Consequence.** Certifying Conjecture 4.1 at `q = 3` discharges the hypothesis of Theorem 4.2 there: **`PΓU(5,3)`
acting on the totally isotropic 1-spaces of `H(4,9)` is non-spreading — unconditionally**, no longer contingent
on an open conjecture. The exact-arithmetic verification extends this evidence to `q = 5, 7`.

## Honest scope

This is verification of an **open** conjecture for small `q`, plus a precise statement of its (implicit)
non-degeneracy — not a proof of the general conjecture, and not a construction. The kernel leg covers `q = 3`
(the smallest field, and the case that makes Theorem 4.2 unconditional); `q = 5, 7` are verified by exact
procedure only (the kernel census grows ~70× per field and is left to the exact decider). The backend is
**report-only**: nothing sets `kernel_verified` or touches `trust.py`; `tests/test_invariants.py` is
byte-identical.
