# Kernel-attested complex Hadamard matrix of order 94 (Szöllősi 2026)

**Date:** 2026-07-06 · **Track:** T5 (external audit) / T9 (external corpus) · **Tier:** audit ·
**EV:** verification-amplification · **Gate:** GREEN (exact integer arithmetic; Lean 4.31 `decide`)

## What this is

An independent, exact-arithmetic confirmation of a 2026 **existence resolution** in a domain new to the ledger
(**complex Hadamard matrices / combinatorial designs**). A *complex Hadamard matrix* of order `n` is an `n × n`
matrix `H` with entries in `{1, −1, i, −i}` and `H H* = n I`. Whether one exists in **order 94** was open;
Szöllősi ([arXiv:2603.09572](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09572), 2026) settles it (**Theorem 1**) via a
Goethals–Seidel-style construction (Theorem 4): four **circulant** `{−1,1}`-matrices `A, B, C, D` of order
`p = 47`, with `A, B` **symmetric** and

$$A A^\top + B B^\top + C C^\top + D D^\top = 4p\, I = 188\, I,$$

assemble (with `R` the back-diagonal) into a complex Hadamard matrix `H` of order `2p = 94`, `H H* = 94 I`.

The hard part is the computer search for the four sequences; **verification is exact and self-certifying** — the
discovering search is *not* part of the certificate.

## What Leibniz verified

Leibniz reconstructs `A, B, C, D` from the length-47 rows printed in the paper — **both** Example 1 **and**,
independently, Example 2 (`0 → −1`) — and verifies by exact integer arithmetic:

- **Published anchors** (a transcription check): row sums `(3,7,7,9)` / `(−1,−5,9,9)` and the summed
  autocorrelation norm `‖Σ‖ = 796` / `1116` (peaks `14` / `18`) match the paper exactly;
- `A` and `B` are **symmetric**;
- **equation (1):** `A Aᵀ + B Bᵀ + C Cᵀ + D Dᵀ = 188 I`;
- the **assembled** matrix `H` (order 94) is unimodular in `{1,−1,i,−i}` and satisfies **`H H* = 94 I`** — i.e.
  it is a complex Hadamard matrix of order 94 (Theorem 1), verified directly, for **both** examples.

## The Lean kernel re-decides the structural core

Since the Gram matrix `G Gᵀ` of a circulant `G` is itself circulant (determined by its first row), equation (1)
is **equivalent** to: the summed periodic autocorrelations of `A, B, C, D` **vanish at every nonzero shift** (and
equal `4·47 = 188` at shift 0) — a length-47 check, far cheaper than the dense `188 × 188` product. The **Lean
4.31 kernel** re-decides that reduced form plus the symmetry of `A, B` — exactly the hypotheses Theorem 4 turns
into the order-94 complex Hadamard matrix — for **both** examples, plus a **negative control** (a single flipped
sign breaks eq (1)), in [`docs/crt/hadamard94.lean`](../crt/hadamard94.lean):

- `had94_eq1_example1`, `had94_eq1_example2` — the summed autocorrelations vanish (⇔ eq (1) = 188 I);
- `had94_sym_example1`, `had94_sym_example2` — `A, B` symmetric;
- `had94_control` — flipping one sign makes eq (1) `false`.

`#print axioms` shows the `eq1`/control theorems depend on **no axioms** and the symmetry theorems on at most
`[propext]` — no `native_decide`, no `sorry`.

## Honest scope

The kernel certifies the finite **structural core** — the sum-of-squares / autocorrelation condition and the
symmetry of `A, B`, which are exactly the finite hypotheses of the paper's Theorem 4 (the assembly into an
order-94 complex Hadamard matrix is a short, cited linear-algebra argument, not re-proved in-kernel). The full
**assembled identity `H H* = 94 I`** is verified **end-to-end by the exact integer procedure** (both examples) —
it is placed there rather than in-kernel because the dense `94 × 94` product hits the plain-`decide` wall
(ADR 0047). The backend is **report-only**: nothing sets `kernel_verified` or touches `trust.py`;
`tests/test_invariants.py` is byte-identical.
